Book miniatures: Adams, The Restaurant at the End of the Universe

I had already written a long, ostentatiously eloquent paragraph about how The Restaurant at the End of the Universe (the sequel to the slightly more famous Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy, which I haven’t read) is the kind of book that requires an attention span of about two and a half minutes and has the benefit of being (both literally and metaphorically) such a light paperback that (as I have confirmed empirically) it can be read in a hammock without any problems. Which is perfectly true, but at that point I noticed that I was being unnecessarily condescending, so here we go again: I enjoyed this book. It’s funny on a micro-level (it doesn’t bother with having more than a rudimentary plot) and it’s kind of smart sometimes. Despite being a science-fiction novel, it’s neither bad nor invests too much energy into social critique. By and large, it’s just plainly silly. I liked it and I might even read Adams again at some point. That’s all. I’m too relaxed to write anything else. I need to get back to my hammock.
