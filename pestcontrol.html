Humane pest control

‘Tis the season! As every year, we are dealing with a rodent situation in our kitchen. We’ve set up humane mouse traps that catch pests without killing them, which is great for the mice, but a bit of a pain for us humans involved. (It turns out that you have to release the mice quite far away from home; otherwise, they’ll find their way back.) Thus, I’ve been contemplating some alternative options for humane pest control. Here are some strategies.

1. Feed the mice an ultra-processed diet. Result: mice develop type II diabetes and cardiovascular diseases and die a premature but natural death, surrounded by friends and family. Problem: still lethal.

2. Offer each mouse $1000 if they willingly board a plane to their place of origin, or alternatively some other location. Result: mice leave kitchen of their own accord. Problem: airline companies might not cooperate.

3. Expose mice to mouse porn in which male mice with unusually long penises penetrate very sexy female mice for unusually long periods of time. Result: male mice develop an inferiority complex and thus chronic erectile dysfunction. Mouse population does not reproduce. Problem: ethically questionable.

4. Expose mice to Buddhist teachings. Result: mice decide to live as hermits far away from civilization and abstain from bodily pleasures. Mouse population leaves densely populated areas such as kitchens, does not reproduce and attains nirvana. Problem: probably low success rate.

5. Provide mice with smartphones. Result: mice loose all social skills because they spend all day on MouseTok and are thus unable to initiate mating relations. Mouse population does not reproduce, but mice are still happily entertained and eventually die a natural death, surrounded by friends and family. Problem: none.
